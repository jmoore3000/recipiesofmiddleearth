Rabbit Stew

What You'll Need

  • A brace of coneys
  • 2 sticks of celery, sliced
  • 3 Tbsp cornflour
  • 1.5 Tbsp butter
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 cups of red wine
  • 2 carrots
  • *Important*4 taters*Important*

Instructions

  1. Dice the meat that you harvested, and coat with cornflower. Shake of any excess
  2. Melt the butter in a pot, then brown the meat. Add the celery, onion and bay leaf â€“ season with salt& pepper, then pour in the water and wine. Bring to a boil, put a lid on, and gently simmer at the lowest setting for 2 hours.
  3. Meanwhile, and dice the taters & carrots. Add these to the stew for the during last 25-30 minutes of the cooking time
  4. When the vegetables are cooked through and the sauce is thick, the stew is ready to serve.

This stew is best served with a sourdoug loaf and a cup of your favorite mead. And remember, there is only one way to serve up a brace of coneys.
